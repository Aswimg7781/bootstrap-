About Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is known for its breathtaking and diverse landscape. The towering Western Ghats run along its eastern border, covered with lush forests, tea gardens, and spice plantations, while the Arabian Sea hugs its western coast with beautiful beaches like Kovalam and Varkala.

TOP DESTINATIONS

Alappuzha

Alappuzha, often called the "Venice of the East," is a scenic coastal town in Kerala, known for its intricate network of backwaters, lagoons, and canals. It is famous for houseboat cruises along the serene Vembanad Lake and the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race. Alappuzha is also home to picturesque beaches, such as Alappuzha Beach, lined with palm trees and an iconic pier. The town is surrounded by lush paddy fields, coconut groves, and traditional coir industries, adding to its rustic charm.

Calicut

Calicut, also known as kozhikode, is a coastal city in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It holds historical significance as a major trade hub, especially during the medieval period, when it was known for its thriving spice trade.With a rich history dating back to the 12th century, Calicut has been a significant cultural and commercial hub

Ernakulam

Ernakulam, the commercial hub of Kerala, is a vibrant urban area and the mainland part of Kochi. Known for its rapid development, it hosts key business districts, including Infopark and SmartCity, making it a major IT and financial center. With excellent connectivity through the Ernakulam Junction and Town railway stations, the Kochi Metro, and proximity to Cochin International Airport, the city is well-connected and bustling with activity
